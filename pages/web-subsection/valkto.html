Escape from Valkto is a school assignment and my partner (Kaiden) and I were tasked to make a text adventure made with HTML, CSS, and JavaScript. The plot of the game follows a monomyth cycle and is based on the sci-fi genre. Before you start the game you will randomly be chosen to start one of the three unique beginnings. Not going in order, one of the beginnings starts you off by crash landing the player(soldier) on a hostile enemy planet with the overarching goal is to find survivors, fight enemies, and call the Mother Ship to come rescue you. Another beginnings starts the player from a perspective of another survivor(Dan) and the starting point is near where the soldier starts off, but after meeting up with the soldier the player's pov shifts back to the soldier. The last beginning is a much slimmer chance of getting, but if the player does get this beginning they automatically avoid the crisis of crash landing on the enemy planet Valkto and the game ends.

One of the main activity we had to do to 'finish' this project was writing it out in Lucid Chart. With Lucid Chart we were able to map out what dialogue choices you had and organize the overall story to flow together more smoothly.

In the end though we were running out of time with dead lines and re-creating a format to have an error free card placement for the dialogue options. Not only did we have a deadline, I feel like both me and my partner ran out of steam for the project near the very end where most of the story needed to be coded into the JavaScript, so the game would work.
