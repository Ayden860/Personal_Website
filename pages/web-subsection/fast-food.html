This project is based off of a hypothetical prompt of “CART(my high school) was hired by Jurassic Park to make a website to connect all fast food chains for ease of use on the island”. My partner Eric and I worked on the fast food section of the website. While the rest of the class worked on different sections that would be on an amusement park website, the end goal for the class was to then bring it all together to have one website. Spoiler alert, at the end all of those websites did not connect with each other.

This was my first ‘major’ project where I had to develop more than 4 pages along with designing the whole thing from scratch. Before this project my instructor was getting us familiar with wireframes, low-fidelity prototypes, high-fidelity wireframes/prototypes, HTML and CSS(this was all in the first semester), not yet getting into JavaScript just yet(we were formally introduced to working with JavaScript in the second semester).

Starting on figma for the overall design of the website, I would say I was the one who mainly made the layout and picked the colors from a limited range of colors(see the image above). I don’t blame my partner for this because if I remember correctly we had a week for the low-fidelity prototype and the next week was for the high-fidelity prototype. I already had a year of experience in UX working in Figma on designs and we needed the work done fast. For the color scheme of the website we were required to only use the colors that Jurassic Park used. A lot of the colors did not really fit the theme of a fast food website, along with conflicting color contrasts. So I chose a monotone green color scheme.

It's been a while and I do not remember in detail, but I do remember that Eric and I were working on this project after school on hours long calls. Looking back, if I were to recode this website, it would take less time and from the many hours of work Eric and I put in, I could probably make this project better than before.
