Near the end of my UX Design year, my class was given the opportunity to have internships with Harris Ranch Resort. There were many projects Harris Ranch gave us (UX students) to do. Some of the projects I remember being listed off were: how to improve on what(their website, calls for food/booking/customor service, and Tesla users) could be more acessible, surveys on Tesla owners and why they do not go into the stores while their Tesla charges, create a map of the resort, online customer reviews, finding pain points when people visit Harris Ranch Resort, making a brochure, redesigning furniture, and many more. These are just some of the many tasks Harris Ranch wanted done.

While doing these tasks, we found pain points that were commonly recurring, so we ideated potential solutions amongst ourselves and then had our teacher (Mrs. Allen) pitch our ideas to Harris Ranch. Some of the ideas Harris Ranch liked and some they rejected. One of the ideas Harris Ranch liked was the food truck called (something) wagon and that was going to be Harris Ranch's temporary shop for Harris BBQ in Bakersfield. Justin the student who pitched the idea found a food truck he had in mind and created a design for the food truck. After seeing Justin's plan, Harris Ranch went with it and immediately bought the food truck and put the idea into motion.

The projects I worked on for Harris Ranch was making (in teams) a brochure for the new Tesla charging stations and finding/ideating pain points from online customer reviews from their BBQ, Resort, Ranch Kitchen, and Prime Steakhouse.

While making the brochure we would design and keep iterating on the design during the week and at the end of the week, Harris Ranch would give us feedback on what they liked and disliked. This kept repeating until Harris Ranch said that they liked the final product.

Near the completion of the brochure, Harris Ranch wanted to rebrand their color and move away from their old green color scheme. So as their last request from us, was to change the brochure colors to their new color scheme.

Overall I enjoyed and learned from this internship with Harris Ranch.
